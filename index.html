My unique career pathway took me from dissecting fruit flies to court rooms selecting jury members to the streets of Accra with mobile micro-lenders.

My Career Progression

Medicine

My interest in medicine started in high school and led me to study biological sciences and healthcare. In addition to doing neurobiology research in epilepsy, I also trained as an EMT.

Law

I was a science consultant for law firms involved in pharmaceutical cases. Using my expertise in epidemiology, I built statistical models for calculating legal settlement amounts.

Business

Completing my MBA allowed me to create better financial models as well as marketing tools for businesses. I could finally add real value to companies hoping to grow using technologies.

Technology

I developed my knack for programming while creating models in Excel. I figured, a programming bootcamp was the perfect place for me to learn how to code and build tools I really wanted.

My Process for Learning and Discovering

Observing

Observation is the first step in the scientific method. To observe means to notice the details. When something looks fuzzy I dive into the details to ask the questions that lead to new discoveries.

Questioning

It is through the process of questioning, seeking answers and asking further questions do I really learn. And sometimes questioning the assumptions can unravel great secrets.

Experimenting

"What gets measured gets done."

I believe experimentation is a great way to measure things to get enough information to make well-reasoned decisions.
